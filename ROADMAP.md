# ROADMAP.md – Decentralized Rights Protocol (DRP)

---

## 1. Vision Statement
> **Empowering individuals worldwide with a decentralized, AI‑driven rights ecosystem.**  
> DRP will give every person sovereign control over their identity, data, and entitlements, enabling transparent, secure, and equitable access to education, health, finance, and other essential services. By integrating cutting‑edge cryptography, post‑quantum security, and cross‑chain AI “Elders,” we aim to become the global backbone for human‑rights‑based digital interactions and to help achieve the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

## 8. Open Challenges

| Challenge | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|-----------|--------|---------------------|
| **Scalability & Performance** | Limits transaction throughput as adoption grows | Implement sharding, layer‑2 state channels, performance benchmarking |
| **Regulatory & Legal Compliance** | Varying data‑sovereignty laws across jurisdictions | Build modular compliance layer, engage legal counsel per region |
| **User Adoption & Education** | Low awareness in target communities | Localized education campaigns, partnership with NGOs, incentive programs |
| **Cross‑Chain Security** | Risks from bridge exploits | Formal verification of bridge contracts, multi‑sig custodians |
| **Post‑Quantum Migration** | Future crypto‑risk | Dual‑signing during transition, community testing |
| **Governance Abuse** | Potential centralization of voting power | Quadratic voting, reputation weighting, anti‑Sybil mechanisms |
| **Data Privacy** | Balancing transparency with privacy | Zero‑knowledge proofs, selective disclosure, GDPR‑style consent flows |
